Episode 7 features Sung Ju Yea, a Python and backend developer based in Tokyo Japan. 

I actually reached out to Sung after stumbling upon his excellent [blog series on Python Async](https://dev.to/uponthesky/series/23690), and I knew right away he had to come on the show. In this episode, we step away from standard syntax discussions to focus on the human side of engineering: the problem-solver mindset. We break down how he transitioned from a background in philosophy and statistics into production backend systems, how he rapidly adapts to brand new codebases across different countries, and his recent deep dive.

**Topics we cover:** <br/>
- **The Adaptability Blueprint:** Landing in new domains, from startups to corporate environments like, and getting productive in weeks. <br/>
- **The Problem-Solver Mindset:** Shifting from just closing tickets to thinking like an architect. <br/>
- **Designing Data-Intensive Applications:** Our take on the [legendary book](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/designing-data-intensive-applications/9781098119058/) by Martin Kleppmann and Chris Riccomini, and applying its data pipeline principles in the real world. <br/>
- **Tech Culture:** The practical differences of working. <br/>
- **AI Engineering for Backend Devs:** Key takeaways. <br/>
